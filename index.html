Kinships are the roots of the African and African American Community.

Historically, our culture values family. Even those not blood related but close to us are called kin. We must preserve our cultural heritage and ancestral traditions and pass them on.

Loving ourselves

See ourselves

To see each other as family, we must stop looking at each other through the lens of white supremacy, oppression, and self-hatred.

Save ourselves

Systematic oppression tore black families apart and strives to break down the black family structure through endorsing stereotypes and misconceptions. Though no family is perfect, to save ourselves we must treat each other like family. We can acknowledge the imperfection and any dysfunction in our community of kinship, and work in love toward doing and being better.

We want to provide a platform for information, to stimulate conversation and to spark action. Besides seeing each other as our family, what other ways are there to create unity?

Uplifting ourselves

OurKinship adopts the ideals of a family unit because an exemplar family:

  • …loves each other
  • …says “I love you” and speaks to each other in kindness
  • …supports each other with time and resources
  • …uplifts and corrects with love, not disparagement
  • …forgives each other
  • …supports each other’s dreams, pursuits, businesses

Education

It is said no one can take your education away. Having an education, whether formal or informal, brings lasting change in reasoning and ability to achieve goals.

Family

White supremacy tried to destroy the traditional black family. During slavery, families were torn apart. Post slavery, black fathers were stripped from their families through lynching and now through mass incarceration.

Environment

Black and brown communities are disproportionately impacted by environmental injustice. Promoting and celebrating environmental awareness in our neighborhoods should be part of our narrative.

BlackLifeHacks

BlacHaxs is our fusion of environmentalism and the culture and ingenuity of black people to uplift the environment and community, preserve resources, and build generational wealth.
